## Restaurant Options

Mix of nice dinners and local iconic spots. Not all need to be used -- pick based on mood and energy each day.

### Nice Dinners

**Sushi Sho** -- One of Waikiki's elite sushi experiences. Omakase is the move. If you can't get in, the bara chirashi takeout is a known fallback.

**The Pig and the Lady** -- Vietnamese-Hawaiian fusion. Chef Andrew Le, James Beard semifinalist 2025. The must-order is the pho French dip. Strong cross-source consensus (Eater, Infatuation).

**Mud Hen Water** -- Ed Kenney, farm-to-table Hawaiian with real local sourcing. James Beard semifinalist 2025. Known for porchetta rolled with lu'au, sausage and clams in coconut broth, banana pancakes, pork sisig at brunch.

**MW Restaurant** -- Hawaii regional cuisine. Less nationally hyped but highly respected locally. Known for mochi-crusted kampachi, miso honey glazed butterfish, and Michelle Karr-Ueoka's seasonal fruit shave ice.

### Local / Iconic / Casual

**Leonard's Bakery** -- Malasadas (Portuguese donuts). Cash only. Unglamorous, completely essential.

**Giovanni's Shrimp Truck** (Kahuku) -- North Shore shrimp trucks are a cultural institution. Best paired with the Day 4 loop.

**Matsumoto's Shave Ice** (Haleiwa) -- Since 1951. Legit Oahu institution.

**Kalapawai Market** (Kailua) -- Great sandwiches, local institution. Natural lunch spot on the Windward coast day.

**House Without a Key** (Halekulani) -- Sunset cocktails, not a dinner spot. Live Hawaiian music, hula dancer. Next door to our hotel. The iconic Oahu sundowner.
