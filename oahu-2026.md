## Day 2 -- Sun Jun 14: Diamond Head + Pearl Harbor

- **6:00am -- Diamond Head Crater Hike** -- 1.8 miles, 560ft gain. Go at opening to beat crowds and heat.
- **Leonard's Bakery** on the way back -- malasadas (Portuguese donuts). Cash only.
- **Pearl Harbor / USS Arizona Memorial** -- late-morning/early-afternoon slot. Free timed entry, must book in advance.
- Back to hotel, decompress
- **Dinner: Mud Hen Water** -- Ed Kenney, farm-to-table Hawaiian. Relaxed vibe.

## Day 3 -- Mon Jun 15: Windward Coast

- Drive **Pali Highway** -- stop at **Nu'uanu Pali Lookout** (dramatic mountain views)
- **Lanikai Pillbox Hike** -- 1.8 miles, moderate scramble. Views of Lanikai and the Mokulua Islands from above.
- **Lanikai Beach** -- small, protected, incredible color. Mokulua Island views.
- **Kailua Beach** -- white sand, turquoise water, more space. 10 min from Lanikai.
- **Kalapawai Market** in Kailua for lunch -- local sandwiches, un-touristy
- Return to Waikiki
- **Dinner:** Casual -- hotel restaurant, poke, or Waikiki neighborhood pick

## Day 4 -- Tue Jun 16: North Shore Loop

- **Giovanni's Shrimp Truck** (Kahuku) for lunch -- North Shore institution
- **Haleiwa town** -- surf culture hub, shops
- **Matsumoto's Shave Ice** -- since 1951
- Return via H-2 or continue the coastal loop
- **Dinner:** Casual Waikiki -- back late, tired from driving

## Ideas & Options

Restaurants and activities we're considering but haven't committed to. Browse over breakfast to plan the day.

### Restaurants

**Nice dinners**

- **Mud Hen Water** -- Ed Kenney, farm-to-table Hawaiian with real local sourcing. Credible, not flashy. Good for a relaxed evening.
- **Vintage Cave** -- 12-course tasting menu, cave-like atmosphere. The most ambitious meal on the island after Senia. $$$$.
- **House Without a Key** (Halekulani) -- not a full dinner spot, but sunset cocktails with live Hawaiian music and hula. The iconic Oahu sundowner. Right next door to our hotel.

**Casual / lunch spots**

- **Kalapawai Market** (Kailua) -- local institution, great sandwiches. Un-touristy.
- **Leonard's Bakery** -- malasadas (Portuguese donuts). Cash only. Hawaii institution.
- **Giovanni's Shrimp Truck** (Kahuku) -- North Shore shrimp trucks are a cultural institution.
- **Matsumoto's Shave Ice** (Haleiwa) -- since 1951. Legit, not a tourist gimmick.

### Activities (still deciding)

- **Kualoa Ranch / Secret Island** -- Secret Island is a private beach only accessible via their tours. Dramatic mountain backdrop, very photogenic, most tourists don't know it exists. Need to confirm availability.
- **Honolulu Museum of Art** -- underrated, genuinely excellent collection, beautiful building. Good rainy-day or slow-afternoon option.
- **Lanikai Pillbox Hike** -- need to confirm trail access (periodic closures).
- **Snorkeling** -- interest level: maybe. Hanauma Bay is the classic spot but crowded and requires reservations. Could also snorkel at Shark's Cove (North Shore, summer only -- calmer water) on the Day 4 loop.
