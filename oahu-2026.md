## Restaurants
{: #restaurants}

<p class="section-intro">Mix of nice dinners and local spots. Not all need to be used -- pick based on mood and energy each day.</p>

<details>
<summary>Nice Dinners</summary>

**Sushi Sho** -- Elite omakase in Waikiki. Intimate counter. If you can't get in, the bara chirashi takeout is the known fallback.

**The Pig and the Lady** -- Vietnamese-Hawaiian fusion. James Beard semifinalist. Must-order: pho French dip. Chinatown location.

**Mud Hen Water** -- Ed Kenney, farm-to-table Hawaiian. James Beard semifinalist. Porchetta with lu'au, sausage and clams in coconut broth. Also strong for brunch.

**MW Restaurant** -- Hawaii regional. Less hyped, highly respected locally. Mochi-crusted kampachi, miso honey butterfish.

**Nami Kaze** -- Nearly swept Honolulu Magazine's 2022 best-new-restaurant. Hamachi crudo, cold-smoked tomatoes with tofu.

**O'Kims** -- Modern Korean. James Beard semifinalist. Confit pork belly brulee, truffle mandoo. Current insider buzz pick.

**Pizza Mamo** -- Brooklyn-style and Detroit-style. Eater calls its pies some of the best on or off the island.

**Bocconcino** -- Deli-panini by day, Neapolitan pizza by night. Mortadella panini with stracciatella and pistachios.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Local / Iconic / Casual</summary>

**Leonard's Bakery** -- Malasadas (Portuguese donuts). Cash only. Hawaii institution.

**Giovanni's Shrimp Truck** (Kahuku) -- North Shore institution. Pairs with the Day 4 loop.

**Matsumoto's Shave Ice** (Haleiwa) -- Since 1951.

**Kalapawai Market** (Kailua) -- Local sandwiches. Natural lunch on the windward coast day.

**House Without a Key** (Halekulani) -- Sunset cocktails, not dinner. Live Hawaiian music, hula. Next door to our hotel.

</details>

<summary>Still Looking For</summary>

More local/casual dinner spots -- plate lunch, poke, izakaya, hole-in-the-wall. Will add as we find them.

## Ideas
{: #ideas}

<p class="section-intro">Curated picks by category. Not a checklist -- grab what fits the mood.</p>

<details>
<summary>Active</summary>

**Lanikai Pillbox Hike at sunrise** -- Best hike-to-view ratio on the island. Sunrise version is uncrowded and the light on the Mokuluas is a different experience. Already in Day 3.

**Hanauma Bay snorkeling (early timed entry)** -- Protected marine reserve inside a volcanic crater. Reef starts in knee-deep water. Book the early slot to avoid crowds.

**Koko Head Crater Stairs** -- 1,048 railroad-tie steps straight up. The serious hike. Fully exposed, go at sunrise. Pairs with a Hanauma Bay morning.

**Kayak to Mokulua Islands from Lanikai** -- ~30 min paddle to a protected cove on Moku Nui. Most tourists stop at the beach. Pairs with post-Pillbox morning.

**Secret Island at Kualoa Ranch** -- Private beach via their tours. Ko'olau mountain wall backdrop. Jurassic Park filming location. Already in Day 4.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Relaxing</summary>

**House Without a Key at sunset** -- Open-air, live steel guitar, hula dancer, Mai Tai. Next door to our hotel. The anti-luau. Already in Day 5.

**Ala Moana Beach Park** -- Where locals go. Calm protected water, wide beach, no tourists. 10 min from hotel. Bring takeout poke.

**Spa day at Halepuna or Halekulani** -- Use the $100 Amex credit at the pool. Check if Halekulani next door offers spa access.

**Early morning Waikiki beach walk (before 7am)** -- Nearly empty, soft light, still water. Free.

**Honolulu Museum of Art** -- 1920s Mediterranean building, open-air courtyards. Monet, Picasso, Hokusai, strong Asian art wing. Good change of pace after days of sun. Day 5 option.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Foodie</summary>

**Senia (booked, Night 1)** -- Chef Chris Kajioka, most respected chef in Hawaii. Counter seating on Saturday = watching the tasting menu composed.

**Poke crawl: Ono Seafood, Maguro Brothers, Fort Ruger Market** -- Three credible spots, rolling lunch. Ono (shoyu ahi, the OG), Maguro Brothers (KCC or standalone), Fort Ruger (neighborhood sleeper).

**Giovanni's Shrimp Truck** -- Scampi is the canonical order, hot & spicy is the sleeper. Day 4.

**KCC Farmers Market (Saturday morning)** -- 7:30-11am. Where locals eat Saturday mornings. Fresh poke, grilled mochi, local fruit. Tight on our Saturdays -- check for midweek alternatives (Blaisdell Wed).

**Leonard's Bakery malasadas** -- The institution locals and food people agree on. Haupia filled + original sugar, eat hot. Cash only. Day 2.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Date Night Dinners</summary>

**Sushi Sho (omakase)** -- Intimate counter, omakase format. In Waikiki, no driving. Reserve early, it's small.

**O'Kims** -- James Beard semifinalist. Modern Korean, inventive. The "in the know" pick.

**The Pig and the Lady** -- Vietnamese-Hawaiian fusion. Pho French dip. More energetic vibe. Chinatown location pairs with gallery walk.

**Nami Kaze** -- Honolulu Magazine near-sweep. Hamachi crudo, cold-smoked tomatoes with tofu. Modern, refined, not stiff.

**Mud Hen Water** -- Farm-to-table. The most "of this place" dinner. Also strong for brunch.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Wildcard</summary>

**Outrigger canoe ride off Waikiki** -- Paddle out, catch waves coming in. Traditional Hawaiian water sport. More intimate than a catamaran. 30-45 min from the beach.

**Yoga class at a local studio** -- Practicing somewhere new in Hawaii. Yoga Hawaii or Still & Moving Center for drop-ins.

**Makapu'u Lighthouse Trail at sunrise** -- Paved 2-mile clifftop walk. Nearly alone at dawn. Views of open Pacific and Rabbit Island. Stacks into a windward coast morning.

**Chinatown evening cocktail crawl** -- Bar Leather Apron (one of the best cocktail bars in the US), Tchin Tchin, Smith & Kings. Pair with dim sum or pho.

**Ka'ena Point (westernmost tip)** -- 5-mile flat coastal trail to the end of the island. Monk seals, albatross, stark landscape. Half-day commitment. The "we walked to the edge" story.

</details>
